Problem

The COVID-19 pandemic has significantly impacted mental well-being, yet mental health remains an often-overlooked issue. A study by the Institute of Mental Health (IMH) during the pandemic found that 8.7% of Singapore’s surveyed population met the criteria for clinical depression, 9.4% for anxiety, and 9.3% for mild to severe stress. Since the implementation of the national Circuit Breaker in 2020, IMH’s Mental Health Helpline has experienced a surge in call volume, highlighting the growing mental health crisis that continues to be inadequately addressed.


Maintaining and enhancing mental health solutions after the COVID-19 pandemic is crucial, as mental well-being remains a vital component of overall health people can benefit from it. By continuing to support these solutions, we can assist individuals dealing with diverse stressors and challenges in their lives. Moreover, the lessons learnt and innovation made in developing mental health responses during the pandemic can be utilised to meet mental health needs in various other situations and crises, highlighting the growing mental health crisis that continues to be inadequately addressed.

Feature Enhancements


Encouraging Messages in Notifications.

Encouraging notifications that pops up on the user’s screen to encourage and care for them. The app may even ask how the users are doing, if they haven’t been online. This is to encourage the users to stay online.

Direct messages.

Sometimes people need listening ears. They might want to look for people to talk to directly. Maybe even someone they don’t know in person.

Journaling.

Journaling can help to prioritise problems, fears, and concerns, as well as track any symptoms on a daily basis to identify triggers and learn how to manage them more effectively. It also provides a space for positive self-talk and allows us to recognize and address negative thoughts and behaviours. This is something that people can use on the app at any time they want, even after covid.

Booking Therapy

Book a session, conduct therapy in person or virtually on the app, with booking, facetime and calling features. This is something that people can use on the app at any time they want, even after covid.
