Game Projects

High Rizz High Reward

"High rizz high reward" is a first-person game where players step into the shoes of a lonely university student. Feeling isolated and in need of a partner, the player visits a robot clinic located within their school. They will read through various documents and profiles of different dating robots, each with their unique personalities and traits. They get to go on a date with their chosen robot. In the year 2050, advanced AI and robotics have seamlessly integrated into society. With the rise of AI, humans can now form deep, meaningful relationships with robots. This game is set in a vibrant New York City, where technology and humanity coexist in harmony.

Entirely modeled.

Penguin Dash

This is a simple endless run 2D game, inspired by the famous T-rex game - Google Offline Run. The purpose of the project is to allow players to sign in/sign up, log out and collect player’s in game data in Firebase.

The UI was designed by me.

MASH AR Application

This project is a AR Application for a Mechanical Keyboard Brand MASH. The app aims to help user choose the keyboard typing sound they like, at the comfort of their home. Example, if they are buying the keyboards on the websites they probably can’t get a first hand experience of how the keys sound like. With this AR experience, they can better choose their switches and also see if their keyboards fits their PC set up aesthetic / how big the product looks in real life.

The UI was designed by me.

Not so Horror Planet

This game takes place on an abandoned planet where your spaceship crash-landed and broke down. You wake up to your companion, “Rudy,” informing you that you’ve crashed into an abandoned planet. The game begins at the crash site, where you explore the wreckage with Rudy and come across a mysterious door. Upon entering, you find yourself trapped inside a factory with no way out. To escape, you must search for keys to unlock doors and gather materials to repair your spaceship. Your journey ends when you successfully fix the ship and make your way back home. As you search for a way out of the factory, you come across two exit signs—one red and one green. Choosing the wrong path leads to a grim fate; stepping through the red exit dooms you to the dangers of the abandoned planet, forcing you to restart your journey. However, if you make the right choice and enter the green exit, you successfully escape.

Entirely modeled, except for robot model.
